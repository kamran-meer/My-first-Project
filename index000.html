Babar Azam

Babar Azam, one of cricket's modern-day stars, has rapidly ascended to the pinnacle of the sport with his elegant batting and consistent performances. Born on October 15, 1994, in Lahore, Pakistan, Babar made his mark in international cricket with his stylish stroke play and remarkable composure at the crease. He debuted for Pakistan in 2015 and quickly established himself as a mainstay in the team across all formats. Known for his technically sound batting, Babar Azam's ability to anchor an innings and accelerate when needed has earned him comparisons to some of the game's greats. He has broken numerous records, including becoming the fastest player to score 1,000 runs in T20 Internationals.

Haris Rauf

Haris Rauf, a dynamic fast bowler from Pakistan, has quickly gained recognition for his blistering pace and ability to deliver in crucial moments. Born on November 7, 1993, in Rawalpindi, Rauf's journey to international cricket is nothing short of inspirational. Initially playing tape-ball cricket, he was discovered by Lahore Qalandars' talent hunt program and soon made his mark in the Pakistan Super League (PSL). His impressive performances in the PSL earned him a spot in the Melbourne Stars squad for the Big Bash League (BBL), where he further showcased his raw pace and wicket-taking ability.

Virat Kholi

Virat Kohli, one of cricket's most prominent and prolific batsmen, has redefined batting in the modern era with his extraordinary skill, consistency, and competitive spirit. Born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, Kohli rose through the ranks of junior cricket, making a significant impact at the Under-19 level before captaining India to victory in the 2008 ICC Under-19 Cricket World Cup. He made his senior international debut in 2008 and quickly established himself as a cornerstone of the Indian batting lineup.
